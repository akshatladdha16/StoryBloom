# Mishika and the Moon Garden Mission (12 Pages)

**Source:** `stories/mishika-draft-2.md`  
**Deliverable:** Page text + illustration brief (max 12 pages)

## Style Notes (Illustrator)
**Target look:** Digital mixed media with soft watercolor light and subtle crayon texture (kid-made energy, but professional finish).  
**Character clarity (6-8):** Big readable expressions, clear silhouettes, not too much background clutter near text areas.  
**Color arc:** Warm morning (gold/green) to windy tension (cooler greens/blues) to sunset and moonlight (peach to indigo with silver accents).

## Page 1
**Page Text:**
On Saturday morning, Mishika laced her shoes and grabbed her sketchbook.
"Today," she whispered, "we're going on a Moon Garden mission."

**Illustration Brief:**
Show Mishika near the doorway with a small bag of crayons and star stickers peeking out. Use a bright, optimistic morning palette. Composition: eye-level, Mishika on the right third looking outward to invite the reader into the adventure; leave a calm sky or wall area for text.

## Page 2
**Page Text:**
In the garden, gulmohar leaves danced above marigolds and mint.
Mishika spread a mat under the tree like a tiny "Mission Control" base.

**Illustration Brief:**
Establish the setting: big tree canopy framing the top, butterflies zigzagging, warm sunlight spots on grass. Add small visual Easter eggs: a chalk star half-finished, a ladybug on a leaf. Perspective: slight bird's-eye to show the mat and the little "base" area clearly.

## Page 3
**Page Text:**
Ayushi arrived first, skipping like a springy comet.
Priya came with juice and snacks.
Chandani brought tape and shiny paper shaped like stars.

**Illustration Brief:**
Group entrance moment. Keep each friend visually distinct with a simple color cue in clothing or accessories (3-4 colors per character). Use leading lines: the garden path and their gaze lines pointing toward Mishika and the mat. Make the mood playful and welcoming.

## Page 4
**Page Text:**
Mishika drew a rocket with four windows.
"One for me," she said, "and one for each of you."
She sketched a map titled: **Moon Garden City**.

**Illustration Brief:**
Close-up with hands and faces visible: sketchbook centered, kids leaning in. Show the rocket drawing with whimsical details (rainbow wing, spoon-shaped windows) without cluttering the page. Text area: keep the top-left clean with soft sky/grass gradient.

## Page 5
**Page Text:**
Then Mishika opened her Barbie box.
"Meet Captain Luna," she said. "She helps us stay brave."

**Illustration Brief:**
Introduce the dolls as a "space crew" without changing their familiar doll look: add tiny DIY helmet bands, sticker badges, a ribbon "jetpack." Composition: dolls in foreground, kids smiling in midground; keep expressions big and readable. Use a pop of silver to signal "space".

## Page 6
**Page Text:**
Boxes became walls.
Rope became seatbelts.
Chalk circles became buttons that said: "BEEP!" and "WHOOSH!"

**Illustration Brief:**
Construction montage on one page: show all four kids doing different tasks so teamwork is clear. Use a dynamic diagonal composition from bottom-left (tools) to top-right (rocket shape). Add texture: visible tape edges, cardboard corrugation, chalk dust.

## Page 7
**Page Text:**
"Mission Control, report!" said Priya.
"Seats ready!" said Ayushi.
"Door guarded!" said Chandani.
Mishika saluted. "Countdown!"

**Illustration Brief:**
Stage them like a real launch: Priya holding the map like a clipboard, Ayushi pointing at seats, Chandani standing proudly by a cardboard door flap, Mishika front-and-center. Perspective: slight worm's-eye to make the rocket feel tall and heroic.

## Page 8
**Page Text:**
"Ten... nine... eight..."
Leaves swirled like tiny galaxies.
"Three... two... one... BLAST!"

**Illustration Brief:**
Windy action scene. Use motion lines and swirling leaves that form a spiral around the rocket. Color shift slightly cooler to emphasize the gust. Page-turn setup: position the swirling leaves leading off the right edge, pulling the reader forward.

## Page 9
**Page Text:**
Their rocket stayed in the garden.
But in their minds, it zoomed past clouds and stars.
"Moon Garden Crater ahead," Mishika announced.

**Illustration Brief:**
Whimsical "imagination overlay" image: keep the real cardboard rocket visible, but layer a semi-transparent starfield and glowing trail behind it. Add a few floating doodles from Mishika's drawing (moon, stars) appearing like real objects. Use purple/indigo accents while keeping faces bright.

## Page 10
**Page Text:**
They hopped over "moon rocks" and planted "stardust seeds."
Then a strong puff of wind snatched their Moon Garden City map.

**Illustration Brief:**
Split the emotional beat visually: kids mid-laugh with chalk and petals, then sudden surprise as the map lifts away. Composition: map high and off-center, creating tension. Keep background slightly simpler here so the flying map is the clear focal point.

## Page 11
**Page Text:**
Ayushi looked worried.
Priya went quiet.
Chandani sighed.
Mishika took a deep breath. "Astronauts stay calm. We search together."
And they did.

**Illustration Brief:**
Show emotion clearly through body language: shoulders droop, eyes wide, hands paused. Then show Mishika steadying the team, pointing gently into different directions. Use a calmer composition with more open space, signaling problem-solving. Add a small hopeful visual: one star sticker stuck on a mint leaf, hinting at the solution.

## Page 12
**Page Text:**
They found the map caught near mint and vine.
They taped it, weighted it with pebbles, and drew Moon Garden City even bigger.
As the moon rose, Mishika whispered, "One day I'll be an astronaut."
Her friends held her close. "Always," they said.

**Illustration Brief:**
Resolution + heart. Sunset-to-moonlight palette: peach at the horizon fading to indigo, with silver moon highlights. Composition: group hug in the foreground, the finished map and Barbie crew arranged beside them like proud helpers. Include re-read details: labeled places on the map (Kindness Station, Learning Dome, Starlight Bridge) and tiny chalk stars leading toward the page edge.

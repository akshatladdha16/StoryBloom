# Mishika and the Moon Garden Mission

**Age Range:** 6+  
**Theme:** Friendship, imagination, teamwork, courage, and dream-building  
**Format:** Expanded rhyming picture-book draft

On a sunny Saturday morning, bright and new,
Mishika tied her laces and knew just what to do.
She packed her box of crayons - pink, silver, blue, and green -
And whispered, "Today, I'll build the best space world ever seen!"

She hurried to the garden where the gulmohar trees sway,
Where butterflies did zigzags in a twirly, twinkly way.
She spread her drawing blanket on a patch of gentle ground
And listened to the happy birds that chirped and chirped around.

Soon Ayushi came skipping with ribbons in her hair,
Priya brought a snack box and juice for all to share,
And Chandani came laughing with stickers shaped like stars.
"Are we ready for adventure?" Mishika asked. "Yes, we are!"

Mishika opened up her book and drew a giant moon,
A rocket with a rainbow wing and windows shaped like spoons.
She drew four tiny astronauts all smiling brave and bright:
"That's me, and you, and you, and you - we'll have a launch tonight!"

Ayushi clapped. "I'll build the seats!"
Priya said, "I'll count the beats!"
Chandani grinned, "I'll guard the door!"
Mishika said, "Let's dream much more!"

Then out came Mishika's Barbie dolls in sparkly astronaut clothes,
With tiny silver helmets and shiny little bows.
"Captain Luna, Doctor Star, and Pilot Rose are here.
They'll help us on our mission when the moon is crystal clear!"

The friends used boxes, tape, and chalk, and branches from the ground,
A skipping rope for safety belts, four pebbles neat and round.
They made a rocket dashboard from an old cardboard tray,
With buttons marked as "WHOOSH!" and "BEEP!" and "MILKY WAY!"

"Mission Control, report!" said Priya, standing tall and straight.
"Rocket ready!" answered Ayushi. "Supplies are looking great!"
"Door is sealed!" said Chandani. "Crew is brave and true!"
Mishika saluted with a grin. "Moon Garden, here we come - woo-hoo!"

They all began the countdown.
"Ten... nine... eight..."
A breeze spun leaves in circles.
"Seven... six... five..."
A squirrel froze, then scampered.
"Four... three..."
A cloud moved soft and slow.
"Two... one... BLAST!"

The rocket didn't move, of course - it stayed right where it stood -
But in their hearts it soared so high, exactly as it should.
They wobbled in pretend space with giggles, squeals, and cheer,
While Captain Luna Barbie said, "The moon is very near!"

At last they "landed" on a stone beside the jasmine vine.
"This must be Moon Garden Crater!" said Mishika. "How fine!"
They drew moon-flowers on the path with chalk in silver swirls,
And planted "stardust seeds" - small beads - like little shining pearls.

But suddenly a problem came, as problems sometimes do:
Their hand-drawn treasure map blew off and tumbled out of view!
It skipped beyond the marigolds and disappeared from sight.
Ayushi frowned. "Without our map, we'll never find Star Light."

Priya felt worried. Chandani sighed.
The breeze kept dancing by.
Mishika took a careful breath
And looked up at the sky.

She said, "Real astronauts stay calm.
They think, then try again.
We are a team. We help each other.
That's how missions win."

So Ayushi checked the bamboo fence,
Priya looked near the tree,
Chandani searched by puddle stones,
And Mishika searched the lea.

They called out clues as they explored:
"I found a feather here!"
"I found two twigs shaped like a V!"
"I found one sparkly earring near!"

Then Chandani shouted, "I see it!
It's caught by mint and vine!"
They cheered so loud the myna birds
Sang back in chirpy time.

Map rescued! Crew reunited!
The mission carried on.
They charted lakes and crater hills
Until the afternoon was gone.

Mishika drew a learning dome
Where children read the stars,
A kindness station, bright and warm,
For anyone with scars,
A friendship bridge from Earth to Moon
With lanterns all aglow,
And gardens where each dream a child
Could plant and help to grow.

Ayushi drew a play park
With swings that touched the sky.
Priya drew a snack cafe
With laddoos stacked up high.
Chandani drew a music hall
Where moon drums boomed with glee.
They laughed, "Our Moon Garden City
Is where all friends can be!"

As sunset painted orange gold
Across the evening air,
The girls sat close in quiet joy
And watched the sky with care.
The first small star winked softly,
Then another, then a few -
Like tiny lights of "You can do it!"
Shining brave and true.

Mishika held her crayons tight
And whispered to the night,
"One day I will be astronaut.
I'll learn, I'll try, I'll write.
I'll study planets, clouds, and winds,
I'll ask and ask and ask.
I'll be kind, strong, and curious too -
I'll do each little task."

Ayushi squeezed her shoulder.
"We'll cheer you every day."
Priya smiled, "We'll build with you."
Chandani said, "Always."

Mishika looked at all her friends,
At dolls lined in a row,
At chalk moons on the garden path
Still softly set aglow.
She knew that dreams grow biggest
When shared with hearts that care,
When friends work side by side with love
And everyone can share.

They packed their things, then turned once more
To wave at moon and tree.
"Goodnight, Moon Garden Mission Base!
We'll come back tomorrow - three?"
"Make it ten!" laughed Priya.
"Make it all day!" Ayushi cried.
"Bring extra stickers!" Chandani said.
Mishika beamed with pride.

And high above, the quiet moon
Shone silver, round, and bright -
As if to say, "Keep dreaming, child.
Your future is in sight."

So if you pass a garden path
Where chalk stars still can gleam,
You might just hear four giggling friends
Designing one more dream -
Of rockets, dolls, and moonlit maps,
Of kindness, joy, and art,
Of little hands that build big worlds
With courage in each heart.

---

## Illustration Notes
- Use warm daylight tones in early scenes, then golden sunset and soft moonlight at the end.
- Keep each friend visually distinct and expressive, with joyful movement and collaborative poses.
- Include recurring motifs: crayons, chalk stars, dolls as mission crew, map, leaves swirling like galaxies.
- Add small re-read details (hidden stars, tiny doodles, playful labels on rocket controls).

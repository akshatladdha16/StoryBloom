# Mishika and the Moon Garden Mission (12 Pages)

**Source text:** `stories/mishika-draft-2.md` (verbatim, only split into pages)  
**Deliverable:** Page text + illustration brief (max 12 pages)

## Global Illustration Direction
- **Audience:** 6-year-olds (early readers / read-aloud)
- **Medium/Style Prompt:** children's picture book illustration, digital mixed media (watercolor wash + colored pencil + subtle crayon-on-paper texture), soft outlines, warm expressive faces, gentle lighting, full-bleed art
- **Character Consistency Prompt:** same character designs across all pages, consistent proportions and outfits, clear silhouettes, big readable expressions
- **Palette Arc Prompt:** warm morning greens/yellows (Pages 1-4), energetic brights + silver sparkle accents (Pages 5-7), cooler breeze/tension (Pages 8-9), warm sunset shifting to indigo moonlight with soft silver highlights (Pages 10-12)
- **Recurring Motifs Prompt:** crayons, sketchbook pages, chalk stars, fashion dolls as "space crew" (Barbie-like but no brand/logo), swirling leaves like galaxies, the flying map
- **Text-Safe Prompt:** leave a calm negative-space area for text (simple sky/grass gradient), do not place busy details behind that area

## Global Character Reference (Use In Every Prompt)
- **Mishika:** 6-year-old Indian girl, medium-brown skin, big expressive eyes, black hair in a high ponytail, teal T-shirt, pink shorts, yellow sneakers, small backpack or crayon pouch
- **Ayushi:** 6-year-old Indian girl, two braids with bright ribbons, orange-and-white outfit, energetic skipping posture
- **Priya:** 6-year-old Indian girl, tidy ponytail, purple top, carrying a snack box and a juice bottle
- **Chandani:** 6-year-old Indian girl, wavy hair, red/yellow outfit, holding star stickers and tape
- **Dolls (Space Crew):** three Barbie-like fashion dolls with DIY paper/silver-band helmets, sticker badges, ribbon jetpacks; avoid any brand marks

## Global Negative Prompt (Use In Every Prompt)
No text, no letters, no watermarks, no logos, no brand names, no photorealism, no scary mood, no gore, no creepy faces, no extra limbs, no extra fingers, no deformed hands, no blurred faces, no messy clutter in the text-safe area

## Page 12
**Page Text (verbatim):**
As sunset painted orange gold
Across the evening air,
The girls sat close in quiet joy
And watched the sky with care.
The first small star winked softly,
Then another, then a few -
Like tiny lights of "You can do it!"
Shining brave and true.

Mishika held her crayons tight
And whispered to the night,
"One day I will be astronaut.
I'll learn, I'll try, I'll write.
I'll study planets, clouds, and winds,
I'll ask and ask and ask.
I'll be kind, strong, and curious too -
I'll do each little task."

Ayushi squeezed her shoulder.
"We'll cheer you every day."
Priya smiled, "We'll build with you."
Chandani said, "Always."

Mishika looked at all her friends,
At dolls lined in a row,
At chalk moons on the garden path
Still softly set aglow.
She knew that dreams grow biggest
When shared with hearts that care,
When friends work side by side with love
And everyone can share.

They packed their things, then turned once more
To wave at moon and tree.
"Goodnight, Moon Garden Mission Base!
We'll come back tomorrow - three?"
"Make it ten!" laughed Priya.
"Make it all day!" Ayushi cried.
"Bring extra stickers!" Chandani said.
Mishika beamed with pride.

And high above, the quiet moon
Shone silver, round, and bright -
As if to say, "Keep dreaming, child.
Your future is in sight."

So if you pass a garden path
Where chalk stars still can gleam,
You might just hear four giggling friends
Designing one more dream -
Of rockets, dolls, and moonlit maps,
Of kindness, joy, and art,
Of little hands that build big worlds
With courage in each heart.
